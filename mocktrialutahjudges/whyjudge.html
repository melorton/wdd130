Why Judge Mock Trial?

Each year we average 110 mock trials throughout the state. Each trial consists of three judges; a head judges and two supporting judges. This means that we need 330 volunteers to help with judging every year! As you can imagine, finding enough volunteers to judge is one of the most challenging aspects of the Mock Trial program. We have worked closely with various colleges as well as the State Bar Association to help making volunteering worth your time.

Benefits of Judging

  • If you are an attorney, it is the easiest 4 hours of CLE credit you can earn.
  • If you are a law student at the University of Utah you receive pro bono credit.
  • If you are a political science or criminal justice student at Weber State, University of Utah, or Utah Valley you can earn extra credit.
  • It is a good opportunity to mingle with other legal professionals.
  • It makes you feel good to contribute to your community.
  • You are helping to educate and influence students about the legal process.
  • Sometimes we give you free food!

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Do you have to have a law background to be a Mock Trial Judge?
A: No. ANYONE who is 18 an older and is willing to volunteer can be a judge.
Q: How long does a trial typically last?
A: 3 hours.
Q: How many trials can I volunteer for?
A: There is no limit.
